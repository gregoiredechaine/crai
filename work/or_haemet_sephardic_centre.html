Or Haemet Sephardic Centre

A highly detailed and complex property that required a heavy level of client engagement.

Client

Or Heamet Sephardic School

Location

Vaughan, Ontario

Size

55 000 sf

Project

Religious Assembly & School

Role

Project Architect & Interior Design

The Or Haemet Sephardic School now known as the Joe Dwek Ohr HaEmet Sephardic School is located just north of Steeles Avenue West on Bathurst Street and is dedicated to traditional Jewish education. The school itself provides education for Pre-School to Grade Eight students in 15 classrooms computer and science labs. The building includes a large gym and swimming pool.

The building is also home to the Sephardic Kehila Centre which has a separate entrance from the school and has a large banquet hall and a two level Synagogue. The hall with associated kitchen can seat 500 people. The Synagogue which can be opened up into the hall is capable of housing 800 participants.

It was the intent of the Centre to reflect in its architecture the vernacular of the civilizations built by Sephardism in the Holy Land and the Iberian Peninsula. It is fitting that the building from out to in is inspired by the Moorish architecture of Cordoba in Spain. The exterior window arches, the interior finishes and Synagogue dome have the colour and traditional feel of the Cordoba architecture.
