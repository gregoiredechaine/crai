Marriot Resort & Casino [Saint Kitts]

A large property in the Carribean was transformed into Marriot's Royal Beach Casino

Client

Royal Group of Companies

Location

Frigate Bay, Saint Kitts

Size

25 Acres - 750 Suites

Project

Hotel, Casino & Building Ammenities

Role

Project Architect [Turner-Fleischer]

Situated along the east side of the island, this 25 acre resort is designed with a central building accommodating 240 guest rooms which includes a 30,000 sf casino, banquet halls, a 14,000 sf SPA and multiple restaurants of varying cuisines. As a family orientated hotel, there is a 5,000 sf indoor area for children and an outdoor play ground. The building also includes meeting rooms, and a large area for retail stores.

On the remainder of the property, nineteen 3 storey blocks house the remaining rooms arrange surround landscaped courtyards and swimming pools. Central to the resort land is the main swimming pool and pool bar and a beach front restaurant. As often seen in Caribbean resorts, an amphitheatre is located close to the main building for shows and nighttime festivities.

The building is framed in concrete and steel with concrete filled plastic panels imported from Canada for the walls. As an added bonus and because utilities are often a challenge on island resorts, the owners built a cogeneration plant providing controlled power and other essential services required to run a resort of the size.
